# Healthcare Data Analysis & Patient Insights

## Objective

The goal of this project was to analyze hospital patient data to uncover insights related to treatment costs, patient outcomes, and overall healthcare performance. The analysis aims to support better decision-making in hospital management and patient care.

---

## Dataset

The dataset contains 984 patient records with the following features:

- Patient demographics (Age, Gender)
- Medical condition and procedure
- Treatment cost
- Length of hospital stay
- Readmission status
- Patient satisfaction score

---

## Tools Used

- Python (Pandas, Matplotlib)

---

## Data Exploration

Initial exploration was performed to:

- Understand dataset structure and data types
- Generate summary statistics
- Identify distributions of key variables

---

## Exploratory Data Analysis

The analysis focused on answering key healthcare questions:

- What are the most common medical conditions?
- What is the average treatment cost?
- Which conditions are most expensive?
- Which conditions require longer hospital stays?
- What is the readmission rate?
- How satisfied are patients overall?

---

## Advanced Analysis

To deepen the analysis, additional techniques were applied:

- **Correlation Analysis** to identify relationships between variables such as cost, length of stay, and satisfaction
- **Group Comparisons** to evaluate differences across gender, readmission status, and medical conditions

---

## Key Insights

- The average patient age is approximately **54 years**, indicating a middle-aged patient population.

- **Cancer and heart-related conditions** are the most expensive to treat and also require longer hospital stays.

- There is a **positive relationship between cost and length of stay**, meaning more severe conditions tend to require more resources.

- There is a **strong negative relationship between age and satisfaction**, suggesting older patients report lower satisfaction levels.

- Patients who were **readmitted have lower satisfaction scores**, indicating possible gaps in treatment effectiveness or follow-up care.

- Treatment costs differ significantly by gender, with **female patients showing higher average costs** in this dataset.

- Certain conditions such as **Heart Attack and Stroke are more common among older patients**, highlighting demographic trends.

---

## Business Impact

This analysis can help healthcare providers:

- Identify high-cost and high-risk conditions
- Improve patient satisfaction and care quality
- Reduce readmission rates
- Allocate resources more efficiently

---

## Conclusion

This project demonstrates how healthcare data can be analyzed using Python to uncover meaningful patterns and relationships. The insights generated can support improved healthcare strategies, better patient outcomes, and more efficient hospital operations.
